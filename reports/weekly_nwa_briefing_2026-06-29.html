Baseten Raises $1.5B Series F at $13B — the AI Inference Layer Is the Week's Hottest Trade

Baseten raised a $1.5B Series F at a $13B valuation, led by Altimeter, Conviction and Spark Capital with Sands Capital and Wellington co-leading — completed across two tranches (at $13B and $11B) and its fourth raise in 18 months, on the back of ~20x year-over-year revenue growth and more than a billion inference requests a day. The clearest sign yet that the money chasing AI has moved from the model labs to the inference layer — the unglamorous plumbing that serves models in production.

Why it matters to Jamie: As an Angel Investor, this is the picks-and-shovels thesis in its purest form — when the flagship category is too capital-intensive to enter, the durable returns hide in the layer everyone running a model has to pay for. As an Applied AI Leader, it's direct architecture intelligence for NWA Intelligence: serving cost and latency are becoming the real moat, and "who hosts the inference" is now a board-level question.

Suggested action: Use Baseten's raise to pressure-test NWA's AI infra sourcing — the inference layer is consolidating fast and richly priced, so the angel edge is in the adjacent plays (eval, observability, cost-routing) the megarounds depend on but haven't yet absorbed. Crunchbase

OpenAI Previews GPT-5.6 (Sol, Terra, Luna) — Under Government-Approved Access Only

On June 26, OpenAI opened a limited preview of its next-generation GPT-5.6 family — flagship Sol, mid-tier Terra, fast/cheap Luna — initially to only ~20 government-approved companies. Sol set a new state-of-the-art on Terminal-Bench 2.1 (agentic coding) and ships with OpenAI's strongest safety stack yet — a posture that, two weeks after the Fable/Mythos export standoff, makes "gated, vetted rollout" the new normal at the frontier.

Why it matters to Jamie: As an Applied AI Leader, a coding-and-agent SOTA from OpenAI is a live benchmark for the Claude-centric stack you build on — but the access model (government-vetted allowlist) is the more strategic signal: frontier capability and frontier availability are now separate variables to architect around. As an AI Educator, the "preview to 20 approved firms" rollout is a ready-made FAU case on how compute and capability are being governed, not just sold. OpenAI

Anthropic Accuses Alibaba of Illicitly Harvesting Claude via ~25,000 Fake Accounts

Anthropic alleged that operators linked to Alibaba's Qwen lab ran roughly 28.8 million queries through nearly 25,000 fraudulent accounts between April and June to harvest Claude's software-engineering and agentic-reasoning behavior — "adversarial distillation" that sidesteps China geo-restrictions — and said it has briefed the White House and senators. (Separately, Commerce this week authorized a limited re-release of restricted Mythos 5 to ~100 vetted US firms and agencies, while Fable 5 stays barred.)

Why it matters to Jamie: As an Applied AI Leader betting NWA Intelligence on Claude as durable plumbing, this is a reminder that the model you depend on is now the target of state-scale extraction and counter-measures — account-integrity and rate-limit regimes will tighten, which can ripple into how you provision your own automated Claude usage. As a Board Director, "is our AI vendor's IP — and our access to it — defensible against distillation" is becoming a real diligence question. Bloomberg

Section C — Trends to Watch

The "Giga VC" Concentration Is Reshaping How Capital Flows

H1 2026 data crystallized a pattern: median seed pre-money up ~19% to ~$16M while seed deal volume fell ~29% YoY — a flight to quality — even as roughly six firms (a16z, Thrive, Lightspeed, Sequoia, Founders Fund, General Catalyst) now dominate startup financing end-to-end, with AI startups commanding a ~$1M premium over the median.

Why it matters to Jamie: As an Angel Investor, when the Giga VCs own the priced, late-stage game and seed gets pricier and more selective, the angel edge moves to the earlier, un-syndicated, off-thesis names. As a Board Director, it reframes follow-on math: if a winner attracts a Giga VC next round, your pro-rata and signaling strategy has to be set before that gravity takes over. Inc.

Enterprise Agents Are Hardening Into a Standard Software Layer

The agent story was about adoption infrastructure, not new models: NVIDIA opened an enterprise AI-agent toolkit with 16+ major software partners (Adobe, Atlassian, SAP, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Siemens), agent-software spend is forecast at ~$206B in 2026 (up ~139% YoY) — yet a Fivetran index found only ~15% of organizations are production-ready while ~60% already spend millions.

Why it matters to Jamie: As an Applied AI Leader, the "everyone's buying, few are ready" gap is both the risk and the opening — the orgs that solve production-readiness capture the budget everyone else wastes, precisely NWA Intelligence's lane. As an AI Educator, that 15%-ready / 60%-spending split is the best slide you can hand FAU leaders to separate hype from operational reality. NVIDIA
