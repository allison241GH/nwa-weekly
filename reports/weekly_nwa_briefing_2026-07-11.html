Norm AI Raises $120M at a $1.2B Valuation — "Agentic Law" Becomes a Unicorn

Norm AI closed a $120M Series C led by Khosla Ventures (with Blackstone, Bain Capital Ventures, Craft Ventures, Coatue, and Vanguard), crossing the $1B mark and reaching unicorn status less than three years and ~$260M raised into its life. Why it matters to Jamie: As an Angel Investor, this is the vertical-application thesis paying off exactly where the moat is defensible — a painful, regulated, expertise-heavy workflow where "agent + human sign-off" beats both pure software and pure headcount, and it's the pattern to hunt for before the megafunds bid it up. Agility Robotics Files to Go Public via SPAC at ~$2.5B — the First Pure-Play Humanoid on US Markets

Agility Robotics — the Salem, Oregon maker of the Digit bipedal warehouse robot, led by CEO Peggy Johnson — announced a merger with Michael Klein's Churchill Capital Corp XI, valuing the company at roughly $2.5B and raising more than $620M in gross proceeds. It would become the first pure-play humanoid robotics company to trade on US public markets — notably, Johnson pointedly declined to promise a robot in your home anytime soon, framing the near-term market as warehouses and logistics.

Why it matters to Jamie: As a former automotive CPO, you've lived the "industrial deployment before consumer dream" sequencing, and Agility hitting the public market on a warehouse-robot business — not a humanoid-in-every-home pitch — is the more honest, more investable version of the embodied-AI story. As an Angel Investor, a public comp finally puts a price on the category, repricing every private humanoid round and sharpening where the durable edge really sits (components, tactile sensing, sim-to-real data) versus the robots themselves.
