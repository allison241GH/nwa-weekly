Section C — Trends to Watch

AI Hardware: Mega-Rounds Before the Product Ships

Brett Adcock (Figure AI → Hark) raised a $700M Series A at a $6B valuation for a company with no public product. Backers include NVIDIA, Qualcomm Ventures, ARK Invest, Intel Capital, and Salesforce Ventures. This continues a distinct pattern: investors are writing checks that would have funded late-stage companies — to proven AI hardware founders — before a single unit ships. The bet is on founder pedigree and the inevitability of ambient AI hardware.

Why it matters to Jamie: As an angel investor, this signals a market bifurcation — mega pre-product rounds for proven founders, a rising bar for everyone else ($2M+ ARR, strong burn efficiency for Series A). Knowing which game you're evaluating in each deal changes your diligence approach entirely.

Enterprise AI Agent Adoption Crossing the Tipping Point

Gartner projects 40% of enterprise applications will integrate AI agents by end of 2026, up from less than 5% in 2025. Capgemini reports 93% of business leaders say companies that scale AI agents in the next 12 months will gain a durable competitive edge. The shift from "AI feature" to "AI infrastructure" is happening at enterprise velocity — not in a future product cycle.

Why it matters to Jamie: As an AI educator and board director, this adoption curve makes your teaching thesis measurable — your FAU students and board companies are entering the year where AI agent implementation separates winners from followers. The window to teach this practically, with empirical data, is now.
