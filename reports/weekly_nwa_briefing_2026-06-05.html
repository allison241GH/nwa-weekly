AI · Public Markets · Angel Investing

Anthropic Confidentially Files S-1 — First AI Lab to File for IPO

Anthropic confidentially submitted a draft Form S-1 to the SEC on June 1, formally launching the IPO process less than a week after its $65B Series H at a $965B valuation. Revenue run-rate hit ~$47B in May (up from ~$10B at year-end 2025), and the filing comes alongside Project Glasswing's expansion of Claude Mythos to 150+ organizations across 15+ countries, including critical-infrastructure pilots and a new Claude Security offering. Anthropic is moving ahead of OpenAI, which is preparing its own confidential filing. [Anthropic] [CNBC]

Why it matters to Jamie: As an Applied AI Leader betting NWA Intelligence on Claude as durable plumbing, this is the strongest possible signal that the model layer is no longer a science project — it's becoming a publicly-traded utility with audited financials, an SEC-supervised disclosure regime, and a forced cadence of forward guidance. As an Angel Investor, Anthropic's transition into the public market resets the comparison set for every AI-native deal you evaluate: private valuations now anchor against a (soon-to-be) listed comp with ~$47B ARR and known unit economics, which makes 2026 vintage deal pricing materially more legible than 2024 was.

Suggested action: Add an "implied public comp" line to your standard NWA diligence template — once Anthropic's S-1 goes public-public, every AI-native deal in your pipeline gets a real comparable, not a hand-waved one.

Worth raising at the next NWA member meeting.

Generalist AI Raises $400M at $2B — The Consensus Physical-AI Bet Forms

Generalist AI closed a $400M round led by Radical Ventures (with USV, 8VC, Norwest, Nvidia, and Bezos Expeditions) at a $2B valuation to build general-purpose AI models for robots operating in factories, warehouses, and labs. Angels in the round include Fei-Fei Li, Naval Ravikant, Eric Yuan, and Bin Lin — the kind of cap-table density that signals category consensus. [SiliconANGLE]

Why it matters to Jamie: As an Angel Investor watching where the smartest money is converging, this is the round to study — when Fei-Fei Li, Naval, and Bezos Expeditions all show up in a single robotics deal, you're looking at the emerging consensus framework for evaluating physical-AI bets. Section C — Trends to Watch

The Series A Bar Keeps Rising — AI Is Now ~80% of Global Venture Dollars

Series A rounds for AI startups now average $51.9M — roughly 30% above non-AI peers — and AI captured 80% of total global venture funding in Q1 2026 ($242B of $300B+). At the same time, the operating-metric bar for an AI Series A is hardening: $2M+ ARR, sub-1.5× burn multiples, 70%+ gross margins, and AI-discoverability ("does your category summary surface you?") is now a real diligence axis. The result: bifurcation — proven repeat founders get pre-revenue mega-rounds; first-timers face a harder gate than at any point in the last decade.

Why it matters to Jamie: Your diligence framework has to know which game it's playing on each deal — repeat-founder mega-round logic and first-timer Series A logic are now structurally different deals. As a board director and AI educator, this is also the data backing for why founder evaluation and burn discipline are the curriculum to lean into.

Enterprise AI Agent Adoption — From <5% to 40% in 18 Months

Gartner now projects 40% of enterprise apps will integrate AI agents by end of 2026, up from less than 5% in 2025. The reinforcing tells this week: Microsoft Copilot Studio expanding, Salesforce Agentforce 360, ServiceNow AI Agents, and IBM/Oracle agent builders all shipping into GA. Anthropic's expansion of Claude Mythos into critical-infrastructure deployments is the same trend at the regulated-industry end of the curve.

Why it matters to Jamie: As an applied AI builder and FAU educator, this is the adoption curve your students and the boards you sit on are now actually inside. The interesting strategic question is no longer "should we adopt agents?" — it's "which workflows are agent-native vs. assistant-augmented?" That's a better-shaped teaching question for the next FAU session.

"Cognitive Debt" Becomes the Counter-Narrative to Agentic Adoption

New research is sharpening the "AI-as-Amplifier Paradox" — initial productivity gains masking "intuition rust" and identity commoditization in expert roles. The BCG self-automator stat (27%) from May has been reinforced this week by fresh academic and Microsoft Research framing that AI usage often suppresses critical engagement rather than augmenting it. The frame that's emerging: human expertise matters more in an AI-powered world, but only if individuals and organizations design for guiding-and-critiquing rather than delegating-and-trusting.

Why it matters to Jamie: As an AI educator and public thinker, this is the most fertile counter-narrative of the year — and one your builder/educator/board credibility is uniquely positioned to occupy. As an angel investor, it's also a real signal on enterprise AI tooling: products that earn long-term wallet share will be the ones that develop their users, not the ones that quietly atrophy them.
