## How to read this

You own shares of **QQQ** and **F**. Selling a covered call against 100 shares collects premium today in exchange for agreeing to sell those shares if the price closes above your strike at expiration. The goal is **dual**: pull in income *and* hold a thin downside cushion, while keeping the strike far enough out-of-the-money (OTM) that assignment stays unlikely. Every candidate below is OTM (~2–5% on QQQ, wider on F), targets a **~0.25–0.35 delta** (roughly a 25–35% chance of finishing in-the-money), and sits in the **30 / 45 / 60-DTE** window where time decay works hardest in your favor.

Prices, premiums, and deltas are **illustrative estimates** off approximate quotes around the June 18 close (markets were shut June 19 for Juneteenth) — confirm the live chain before you trade. Two wrinkles this week: QQQ ripped +2.5% on Thursday in a post-FOMC relief rally back toward its highs (so premium for the same delta is leaner), and Ford has slid ~20% off its late-May level, which fattens its premium-as-a-percent but means you'd be writing calls on a name still in a downtrend.

- **Income + protection read:** With QQQ near highs and IV compressed, the **$762 / 45-DTE** leg is the balanced pick — ~1.9% of premium for a strike ~3% out, leaving room for the rally to keep running before you'd be called. If you're more protective after the FOMC whipsaw, the **$755 / 30-DTE** pays the fastest theta; if you think the melt-up has further to go, the **$772 / 60-DTE** keeps the most upside.
- **⚠️ Ex-dividend flag:** QQQ's next ex-dividend is **~June 22** (small quarterly distribution, ~$0.59), falling inside all three windows but only ~3 days out. The rule still holds — *never let a covered call sit in-the-money into an ex-dividend date you care about*, because an ITM call can be assigned early the day before ex-div so the counterparty captures the distribution. At 1.9%+ OTM you're not ITM today, and at ~$0.59 the dividend is too small to make early assignment economically attractive on its own — so the practical risk here is low. The *next* ex-div after this one is mid-to-late September; none of these expiries straddle it.

## This week's framing

- **If your priority is income:** lean to the **30-DTE** legs ($755 QQQ, $15 F) — fastest theta and highest annualized yield. Ford's elevated IV means its $15 strike is the richest premium-per-dollar on the board this week.
- **If your priority is keeping upside:** the **60-DTE, further-OTM** legs ($772 QQQ, $16 F) cap you only on a sharp continued move, at the cost of a slower premium drip — the right call on QQQ if you think new highs keep coming.
- **The QQQ ex-div (~June 22) is a non-event this week** at ~$0.59 and with strikes comfortably OTM — note it, don't fear it.
- **Ford is the judgment call.** Rich premium is seductive, but you're selling calls on a downtrend; size it as income on shares you intend to hold, not as a way to "make back" the slide — and remember the premium is taxed as a short-term gain (this week's Options Learning lesson).
