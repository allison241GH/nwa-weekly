## F (Ford) — ~$13.36 (still depressed, earnings and ex-div both loom)

Ford is trading around $13.36, still working off the spring selloff. Two scheduled events sit *inside* the option windows and dominate the calculus this cycle: **Q2 earnings (~July 29, unconfirmed)** and the **regular $0.15 quarterly dividend going ex ~Aug 11**. Critically, earnings land *before* even the 30-DTE expiry — so unlike a normal month, there is no "safe" standard window here; a genuinely event-free Ford write has to expire *before ~July 29*. Ford's $0.50 strike grid is coarse at a ~$13 share price, so the practical OTM choices are **$14.00** (~4.8%) and **$14.50** (~8.5%).

**Income vs. protection read:** Ford's premiums are small in dollar terms but its static *yields* are richer than QQQ's because IV is elevated on a beaten-down, event-heavy name — that's the market paying you to hold a stock it's nervous about. The catch: *every* window in the table straddles earnings, and the two back-months also straddle the ex-dividend, so those higher if-called returns come with real path risk. This is a name to write deliberately, not on autopilot.

**Ex-div / event flag (READ THIS):**
- **Earnings ~July 29** falls *before* the 30-DTE (~Aug 1) expiry and *inside* both back-month windows → expect an IV crush and gap risk. There is no event-free standard window this cycle. Do not write across earnings unless you specifically intend to hold through the print.
- **$0.15 ex-dividend ~Aug 11** falls *inside* the 45-DTE (~Aug 15) and 60-DTE (~Sep 1) windows. The rule holds: **never let a covered call sit in-the-money into an ex-dividend date you care about** — an ITM call can be assigned *early* the day before ex-div to capture the dividend. With OTM strikes ($14.00/$14.50) and Ford at ~$13.36, assignment risk is low *today*, but a post-earnings pop toward your strike before Aug 11 flips that quickly — be ready to roll up-and-out or close.
- A **~July 24** expiry would clear *before* earnings and the ex-div if you want a clean, event-free Ford write this cycle — accepting that a sub-two-week write books only a sliver of premium.
