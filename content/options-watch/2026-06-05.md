## How to read this

You own shares of **QQQ** and **F**. Selling a covered call against 100 shares collects premium today in exchange for agreeing to sell those shares if the price closes above your strike at expiration. The goal here is **dual**: pull in income *and* give yourself a small downside cushion, while keeping the strike far enough out-of-the-money (OTM) that assignment stays unlikely. Every candidate below is OTM (~2–5%), targets roughly a **0.25–0.35 delta** (a rough ~25–35% chance of finishing in-the-money), and sits in the **30 / 45 / 60-DTE** window where time decay works hardest in your favor.

Premiums and deltas are **illustrative estimates** from approximate prices — confirm the live chain before you trade. The chip-led Friday selloff has lifted IV modestly off the recent floor, so call premium is slightly richer than last week's Watch.

- **Income + protection read:** With IV slightly off the floor, the **$755 / 30-DTE** call is the cleanest income trade — ~1.7% per month from a strike just above the prior week's price area, with a ~$12.50 premium cushion under the shares. The **$770 / 60-DTE** leg trades faster theta for more headroom on a rebound; if you think Friday's chip drawdown was a one-week event, that's the leg that lets you keep more of the bounce.
- **⚠️ Ex-dividend flag:** QQQ's next ex-dividend is **~June 22** (small quarterly distribution, projected ~$0.50–$0.76). It falls **inside the 30- and 45-DTE windows**. The rule still applies: never let a covered call sit in-the-money into an ex-dividend date you care about. At 1.9–2.6% OTM the assignment risk is modest, but if QQQ rallies 2%+ back into your strike before June 22, watch for early assignment the day before ex-div.

- **⚠️ Ex-dividend flag:** F's next ex-dividend is **~Aug 11** ($0.15 regular). It *becomes* a concern if you roll out to a **September** expiry — an ITM call would then straddle the Aug 11 ex-div, and a counterparty may exercise early the day before to capture the $0.15. Roll up-and-out *before* ex-div, or pick a strike comfortably OTM, to avoid surrendering the dividend.

## This week's framing

- **If your priority is income:** lean to the **30-DTE** legs ($755 QQQ, $17 F) — fastest theta, highest annualized yield, and a friendlier IV setup than last week's all-time-high tape.
- **If your priority is protecting the shares from another chip-led leg lower:** the premium itself *is* your cushion; nearer strikes pay more for that protection. The $755 QQQ / 30-DTE pays ~$12.50/share against a ~$740 cost basis.
- **If you want to keep most of the upside on a bounce:** the **60-DTE, further-OTM** legs ($770 QQQ, $18 F) cap you only on a sharp recovery, at the cost of a slower premium drip.
- **The standing rule:** never let a call sit in-the-money into an ex-dividend date you care about. QQQ (~Jun 22) is the only ex-div inside these windows; F (~Aug 11) only matters if you roll into September.
