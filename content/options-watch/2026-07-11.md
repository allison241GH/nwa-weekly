## F (Ford) — ~$14 (up ~3% Friday; earnings and ex-div now inside every window)

Ford closed the week around **$14**, up more than 3% on Friday. Two scheduled events sit *inside* the option windows and dominate the calculus: **Q2 earnings on Tuesday, July 28** (results 4:05 p.m. ET) and the **regular $0.15 quarterly dividend going ex on Aug 11** (payable Sept 2; no supplemental announced this cycle). Critically, earnings land *before* even the 30-DTE expiry — so unlike a normal month, **there is no "safe" standard window here**; a genuinely event-free Ford write has to expire *before ~July 28*. Ford's $0.50 strike grid is coarse at a ~$14 share price, so the practical OTM choices are **$14.50** (~3.6%) and **$15.00** (~7.1%).

**Income vs. protection read:** Ford's premiums are small in dollar terms but its static *yields* are richer than QQQ's because IV is elevated on a beaten-down, event-heavy name — the market paying you to hold a stock it's nervous about, now with a post-earnings catalyst in view. The catch: *every* window in the table straddles the July 28 print, and the two back-months also straddle the Aug 11 ex-dividend, so those higher if-called returns come with real path risk. This is a name to write deliberately, not on autopilot.

**Ex-div / event flag (READ THIS):**
- **Earnings July 28** falls *before* the 30-DTE (~Aug 14) expiry and *inside* both back-month windows → expect an IV crush and gap risk. There is no event-free standard window this cycle. Do not write across earnings unless you specifically intend to hold through the print.
- **$0.15 ex-dividend Aug 11** falls *inside* the 45-DTE and 60-DTE windows. The rule holds: **never let a covered call sit in-the-money into an ex-dividend date you care about** — an ITM call can be assigned *early* the day before ex-div to capture the dividend. With OTM strikes ($14.50/$15.00) and Ford at ~$14, assignment risk is low *today*, but a post-earnings pop toward your strike before Aug 11 flips that fast — be ready to roll up-and-out or close.
- A **~July 24** weekly expiry would clear *before* earnings and the ex-div for a clean, event-free Ford write this cycle — accepting that a sub-two-week write books only a sliver of premium.
