## QQQ — ~$707 (off the ~$745 early-June record, choppy)

QQQ has drifted down ~5% from its June 2 high in a tech-led rotation, which *raises* implied volatility — and richer IV means fatter call premiums for the same strike distance. The most recent quarterly distribution already went ex on ~June 22; the **next ex-dividend is ~Sept 21**, which sits *outside* all three windows below. That makes this a clean setup with **no early-assignment dividend risk** to manage on QQQ.

**Income vs. protection read:** With no ex-div in any window, QQQ is purely an income-vs-upside decision. The 30-DTE $725 maximizes premium-per-day (theta) and keeps you nimble if the rotation reverses and tech rips back; the 60-DTE $735 books more total premium and a higher if-called ceiling but rents out two months of any rebound. In a choppy-to-sideways tape, the shorter-dated, slightly-closer strike is the higher-probability income trade.

**Ex-div flag:** ✅ No QQQ ex-dividend falls inside the 30/45/60-day windows (next is ~Sept 21). No dividend-capture early-assignment risk on these expiries.

## F (Ford) — ~$14.13 (down from mid-$17 in late May, stabilizing)

Ford slid hard on weak May U.S. sales (-13.6% YoY) and a heavy recall overhang, then steadied near $14 this week. Two scheduled events sit *inside* the option windows and change the calculus: **Q2 earnings (~July 29, unconfirmed)** and the **regular $0.15 quarterly dividend going ex ~Aug 11**. Ford's $0.50 strike grid is coarse at a ~$14 share price, so the practical choices are the defensive **$14.50** (~2.6% OTM) and the more upside-friendly **$15.00** (~6% OTM).

**Income vs. protection read:** Ford's premiums are small in dollar terms but its static *yields* are richer than QQQ's because IV is elevated post-selloff — that's the market paying you to hold a name it's nervous about. The catch is that both back-month windows straddle earnings *and* the ex-dividend, so the higher if-called returns come with real path risk.

**Ex-div / event flag (READ THIS):**
- **Earnings ~July 29** falls *before* the 30-DTE July 31 expiry and *inside* both back-month windows → expect elevated IV and gap risk. Do not write a call across earnings unless you specifically intend to.
- **$0.15 ex-dividend ~Aug 11** falls *inside* the 45-DTE (Aug 14) and 60-DTE (Aug 28) windows. The rule holds: **never let a covered call sit in-the-money into an ex-dividend date you care about** — an ITM call can be assigned *early* the day before ex-div to capture the dividend. With OTM strikes ($14.50/$15.00) and Ford at ~$14, assignment risk is low *today*, but if a post-earnings pop pushes Ford toward your strike before Aug 11, be ready to roll up-and-out or close.
- A **~July 18** expiry would clear *before* both earnings and the ex-div if you want a clean, event-free Ford write this cycle.
