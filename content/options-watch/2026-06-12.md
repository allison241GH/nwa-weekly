## How to read this

You own shares of **QQQ** and **F**. Selling a covered call against 100 shares collects premium today in exchange for agreeing to sell those shares if the price closes above your strike at expiration. The goal is **dual**: pull in income *and* hold a small downside cushion, while keeping the strike far enough out-of-the-money (OTM) that assignment stays unlikely. Every candidate below is OTM (~2–5%), targets a **~0.25–0.35 delta** (roughly a 25–35% chance of finishing in-the-money), and sits in the **30 / 45 / 60-DTE** window where time decay works hardest in your favor.

Prices, premiums, and deltas are **illustrative estimates** off approximate quotes — confirm the live chain before you trade. The key wrinkle this week: this was a melt-up that carried QQQ back toward its highs, so implied vol compressed again (thinner premium) *and* QQQ is now near a **June 22 ex-dividend** — which makes the "roll before ex-div" decision the live one.

- **Income + protection read:** With QQQ near highs and IV compressed, the **$772 / 45-DTE** leg is the balanced pick — ~1.9% of premium for a strike ~2.8% out, leaving some room for the rally to keep running before you'd be called. If you're more protective after last week's whipsaw, the **$765 / 30-DTE** pays the fastest theta; if you think the melt-up has further to go, the **$782 / 60-DTE** keeps the most upside.
- **⚠️ Ex-dividend flag (the live one this week):** QQQ's next ex-dividend is **~June 22** (small quarterly distribution, ~$0.55–$0.75). It falls **inside all three windows** and is only ~10 days out. The rule: *never let a covered call sit in-the-money into an ex-dividend date you care about* — an ITM call can be assigned early the day before ex-div so the counterparty captures the distribution. At 1.9–4.1% OTM you're not ITM today, but QQQ is close enough that a 2%+ pop before June 22 would put the $765 strike in play. If that happens, **roll up-and-out before June 22** rather than risk losing the shares and the dividend (this is exactly this week's Options Learning lesson).
