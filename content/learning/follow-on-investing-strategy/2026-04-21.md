Follow-on investing is the decision to put more money into a company you've already backed — typically in a subsequent funding round. It sounds simple, but it's one of the most psychologically treacherous decisions in angel investing, because your prior investment creates a built-in bias toward saying yes again.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: most angel returns come from a tiny number of winners (the power law). Follow-on investing is the mechanism that lets you increase your stake in those winners before the price gets prohibitive. But it only works if you treat each follow-on as a brand-new investment decision. The question isn't "I already put in $50K, should I put in more?" — it's "If I were seeing this company for the first time at this valuation, with this traction, would I invest?" If the answer is no, your first investment is a sunk cost, not a reason to keep going.

The best VCs reserve 50–70% of their fund for follow-ons. As an angel, you may not have that luxury, but you should mentally earmark at least some capital for your best performers. The worst mistake is being fully deployed with no reserves when your breakout company raises its next round and offers you pro-rata rights.

## Key Terms

**Pro-Rata Rights** — The contractual right to invest in a future round to maintain your ownership percentage. If you own 2% and the company raises a Series A, pro-rata rights let you invest enough to keep your 2%. Without them, you get diluted. Negotiate for these in every deal.

**Reserve Ratio** — The percentage of total investable capital you set aside for follow-on rounds vs. initial investments. A common angel framework is 60% for new deals, 40% reserved for follow-ons — though this depends on your portfolio size and deal flow volume.

## Real-World Example

Look at what Amazon just did with Anthropic. They invested $8B initially, saw the company reach $380B valuation with clear product-market fit and an expanding moat (Claude, Mythos, AWS integration), and committed up to $25B more — but with milestone-based tranches. That's disciplined follow-on investing at hyperscaler scale. The principle is identical at the angel level: follow on into your winners, but tie additional capital to demonstrated progress, not just optimism.
