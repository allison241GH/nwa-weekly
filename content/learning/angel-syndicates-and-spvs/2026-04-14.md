An **angel syndicate** is a group of individual angel investors who pool capital to make a single investment, typically led by a "syndicate lead" who sources the deal, performs diligence, and negotiates terms. A **Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV)** is the legal entity — usually an LLC — created to hold that pooled investment. The syndicate is the group; the SPV is the wrapper.

Why this matters for angel and seed investors specifically: As today's Q1 data shows, mega-rounds are pricing angels out of frontier AI. But syndicates and SPVs give angels leverage in the application-layer deals where you do have access. A solo angel writing a $25K check may not get a board seat or meaningful information rights. But a syndicate writing $250K–$500K through an SPV can negotiate pro-rata rights, information rights, and even observer seats — punching well above any individual member's weight class.

The coaching insight that doesn't come from business school: The most valuable thing a syndicate lead brings isn't capital — it's deal flow curation and post-investment support. The best leads say "no" to 95% of deals and add operational value to the 5% they fund. Bad syndicate leads optimize for volume (more deals = more carry). Ask yourself: does this lead have a thesis, or are they just a funnel? And critically — who else is in the syndicate? A syndicate with operators who can help the founder is worth 3x one filled with passive check-writers.

## Key Terms

**SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle)** — A single-purpose LLC created to make one specific investment. Investors become limited partners. The lead (GP) manages the entity and typically earns 15-20% carried interest on profits plus a small management fee.

**Carried Interest ("Carry")** — The syndicate lead's share of profits from a successful exit — typically 15-20%. This is the primary economic incentive for the lead. It only pays out when the investment returns capital above the original amount.

## Real-World Example

AngelList pioneered the modern syndicate model. When Calm (the meditation app) was raising its Series A in 2018, several angel syndicates pooled capital through SPVs to participate alongside Insight Partners. Individual angels in those syndicates wrote checks as small as $1K, but the combined SPV commanded a $500K allocation with information rights. When Calm hit a $2B valuation by 2020, those syndicate members saw 20x+ paper returns — minus the 20% carry to their syndicate lead. The lead made the deal accessible; the SPV made it investable at scale.
