### Marc Andreessen Joins the Fed — a16z's Co-Founder to Co-Lead an AI, Productivity & Jobs Task Force

New Fed Chair **Kevin Warsh named a16z co-founder Marc Andreessen** to co-lead a new external **"Productivity and Jobs" task force** — alongside Stanford economist Charles I. Jones and Microsoft EVP Asha Sharma — charged with evaluating how AI is reshaping employment, productivity, and growth, with recommendations due by the end of 2026. It is a striking move: one of venture's most outspoken AI accelerationists is now seated inside the central bank's policy-formation tent.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Angel Investor, this is a signal about where the *narrative* — and eventually the policy tailwinds and rate expectations — around AI-driven productivity gets set; when a16z's worldview is being piped directly into the Fed's thinking, the "AI is deflationary and expansionary" thesis that underwrites frontier valuations gets institutional air cover. As an AI Educator, it's a ready-made FAU discussion on the blurring line between venture ideology and macro policy — and a rare case where an investor's public thesis becomes a governance input.

**Suggested action:** Watch what this task force says about *jobs* specifically — if it leans toward "augmentation, not replacement," expect it to be cited in every AI-labor debate through 2027, and it's worth having your own view ready for board and classroom conversations.

### Norm AI Raises $120M at a $1.2B Valuation — "Agentic Law" Becomes a Unicorn

**Norm AI closed a $120M Series C led by Khosla Ventures** (with Blackstone, Bain Capital Ventures, Craft Ventures, Coatue, and Vanguard), crossing the $1B mark and reaching **unicorn status** less than three years and ~$260M raised into its life. The company builds agentic systems that draft and review legal and compliance documents under attorney supervision — a clean example of AI agents moving up into a high-value, regulated professional workflow.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Angel Investor, this is the vertical-application thesis paying off exactly where the moat is defensible — a painful, regulated, expertise-heavy workflow where "agent + human sign-off" beats both pure software and pure headcount, and it's the pattern to hunt for before the megafunds bid it up. As a Board Director, agentic legal/compliance tooling is quietly becoming a governance question of its own: who is accountable when an agent drafts the filing?

### Agility Robotics Files to Go Public via SPAC at ~$2.5B — the First Pure-Play Humanoid on US Markets

**Agility Robotics** — the Salem, Oregon maker of the **Digit** bipedal warehouse robot, led by CEO Peggy Johnson — announced a merger with Michael Klein's **Churchill Capital Corp XI**, valuing the company at roughly **$2.5B** and raising more than **$620M** in gross proceeds. It would become the **first pure-play humanoid robotics company to trade on US public markets** — notably, Johnson pointedly declined to promise a robot in your home anytime soon, framing the near-term market as warehouses and logistics.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As a former automotive CPO, you've lived the "industrial deployment before consumer dream" sequencing, and Agility hitting the public market on a warehouse-robot business — not a humanoid-in-every-home pitch — is the more honest, more investable version of the embodied-AI story. As an Angel Investor, a public comp finally puts a price on the category, which reprices every private humanoid round and sharpens where the durable edge really sits (components, tactile sensing, sim-to-real data) versus the robots themselves.

## Section C — Trends to Watch

### The AI Unicorn Machine Is Running Twice as Hot as Last Year

TechCrunch's running tracker shows **nearly 90 startups crossed the $1B mark in the first half of 2026 — more than double last year's pace** — overwhelmingly AI-driven, with standouts like Bezos-linked Prometheus ($41B valuation) and the UK's Ineffable Intelligence ($5.1B). Unicorn minting has stopped being a milestone and become a weekly event.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Angel Investor, a doubling in unicorn velocity is both opportunity and warning — the paper markups are real, but so is the entry-price inflation, and the angel edge is increasingly about getting in *before* a company is "obviously" a unicorn-in-waiting, because by the time it is, the round is a megafund's. As a Board Director, it reframes exit math: a $1B private mark means far less in a market where 90 companies a half-year carry one, so liquidity discipline matters more than headline valuation.

### Robotaxis Cross From Pilot to Commercial Footprint

Autonomy went from demos to geography this week: **Waymo announced driverless expansion into four new cities** (San Diego, Las Vegas, Tampa, Denver) on its way to a targeted 1M weekly trips, **Tesla launched an unsupervised robotaxi service in Miami** (deliberately testing vision-only in hurricane-season rain), and **China's Momenta listed in Hong Kong**, raising ~$751M with cornerstones including BlackRock, Fidelity, GIC, Mercedes-Benz, and BYD. The story is no longer "does it work" but "how fast does the map fill in."

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As a former FordPass CPO, this is the inflection you spent years anticipating — the shift from safety-driver pilots to genuine commercial coverage — and the more strategic read is that the *supplier and public-market* layer (Momenta's cornerstone list) is now where global auto and finance incumbents are placing autonomy bets. As an Angel Investor, watch the second-order plays: fleet ops, remote assistance, insurance, and mapping are the picks-and-shovels that every robotaxi network has to buy.

### New-Fund Formation Is Quietly Pivoting to "Frontier Tech"

The fund-of-funds signal this week: **Paradigm closed a $1.2B fourth fund and openly rebranded as a "technical frontier" investor** chasing AI and robotics alongside crypto, while **Chemistry is raising a $500M second fund** — evidence that LP capital is still flowing to emerging and specialist managers, but increasingly under an AI/robotics-first banner rather than a single-sector one.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Angel Investor and NWA participant, this is the competitive backdrop for your own sourcing — when even crypto-native funds are pivoting to "frontier tech," the AI/robotics deal you like will have more, and better-capitalized, bidders, so your differentiation has to be conviction and access, not just being early. As a Board Director, more specialist dry powder means portfolio companies have real optionality on who leads the next round — a lever worth using deliberately.

## Section D — Learn and Watch

- **📝 Read — "Is AI ruining our skills? Early results are in — and they're not good" (Nature, July 5)** — Nature's Mariana Lenharo pulls together the first real evidence of AI-driven *deskilling*: medical and computer-science students who habitually leaned on AI performed measurably worse when tested without it, 77% of physicians and 70% of nurses report worrying about skill loss, and an Anthropic RCT of 52 engineers gets cited on both sides. Scientific American and the APA's Monitor ran companion analyses the same week. This is the sharpest current framing of *what human expertise is still worth* — and a ready-made spine for an FAU lecture on using AI without atrophying the judgment it's supposed to augment.
