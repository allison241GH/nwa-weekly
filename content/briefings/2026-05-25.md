## Section A — Top Stories of the Week

### Markets and Macro

The 30-year Treasury yield hit 5.19% on Monday — its highest level since before the 2008 financial crisis — as markets repriced the Fed's timeline amid persistent fiscal concerns and a surprise deterioration in the US credit outlook. The S&P 500 retreated from its recent record highs mid-week (closing around 7,353), ending what had been an 8-consecutive-week winning streak; the Nasdaq also dropped roughly 0.84%.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** The 5.19% long-end yield is a "higher for longer" signal with teeth — if you're holding any long-duration treasuries (20–30 year), this week's move compressed mark-to-market value meaningfully; it's worth verifying your duration positioning matches your rate outlook before the next portfolio review.

### Modal Labs Raises $355M Series C — AI Infrastructure Is the Real Trade

Modal Labs closed a $355M Series C at a $4.65B valuation (led by General Catalyst and Redpoint), disclosing that annualized revenue crossed $300M — up 5x from $60M just eight months ago. Modal provides serverless GPU access and AI sandbox infrastructure; sandboxes alone drive more than a third of revenue.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** Modal is the clearest signal yet that AI infrastructure — not AI apps — is where the durable capital returns are accumulating. As an angel investor, this is the "AWS vs. dot-com" moment: the compute and sandbox layer is scaling faster than any prior infrastructure cohort, and it's being priced before most seed investors have updated their mental models.

**Suggested action:** Add Modal-adjacent companies — serverless inference, AI sandboxing, GPU routing — to your NWA deal flow watchlist. These are hitting revenue scale and capital efficiency faster than application-layer AI plays.

### Anthropic's "Code w/ Claude" London Event — Dreaming Agents Change the Architecture

Anthropic held its first European developer event ("Code w/ Claude") in London this week, announcing the "dreaming" feature: Claude Code agents now write memory notes to themselves mid-task that other agents can read to coordinate, learn from prior errors, and accelerate onboarding to a codebase. Anthropic also disclosed that Claude now performs at the level of a mid-level software engineer on coding tasks.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** "Dreaming" is the first native multi-agent memory primitive in Claude Code — it transforms agent sessions from stateless sprints into cumulative institutional knowledge. If you're building agentic workflows on top of Claude Code for NWAI or teaching AI orchestration at FAU, this is the architectural shift that makes persistent agent teams viable.

**Suggested action:** Test the dreaming feature on a bounded NWAI task — it directly addresses the cold-start problem in long-running agent workflows and could meaningfully reduce ramp time for new agent sessions.

## Section C — Trends to Watch

### AI Cognitive Debt: Are Your Employees Getting Dumber?

A BCG follow-up study of 244 consultants identified three AI collaboration modes: **Cyborgs** (60%) engage in iterative AI dialogue and build expertise; **Centaurs** (14%) use AI selectively and deepen domain knowledge; **Self-Automators** (27%) delegate entire workflows and develop neither AI skills nor domain expertise. Researchers call the Self-Automator pattern "cognitive debt" — performance holds in the short term while the underlying capacity quietly degrades. The crucial distinction from prior automation: AI offloads *reasoning itself*, not just computation or navigation.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** This is directly teachable at FAU Executive Education — executives need to consciously choose the Cyborg mode, not default into Self-Automation. The BCG framework gives you a crisp diagnostic model for an entire AI for Business Leaders session.

---

### AI Infrastructure Capital Is Clustering — The Deployment Gap Bet

$5.5B in VC capital was deployed this quarter specifically targeting what investors are calling the "deployment gap" in enterprise AI — the distance between capability and production-grade deployment. Modal ($355M), Rhoda AI ($450M public launch), and multiple infrastructure-focused rounds confirm this isn't a single bet; it's an emerging consensus among top-tier investors that the compute, sandbox, and inference layer is the durable value creation layer.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** At the seed/angel stage, you're likely evaluating application-layer AI companies. But the infrastructure-layer premium is now undeniable — this is worth factoring into your investment thesis for NWA deals: what infrastructure does this startup depend on, and is that infrastructure moat defensible or commodity?

---

### The Series A Bar Has Moved — Seed Companies Need to Know

2026 Series A investors are now requiring $2M+ in ARR and $0.50 burn per $1 of new ARR — significantly higher than 2023–2024 benchmarks. The practical implication: companies raising seed rounds today need more runway and a higher growth bar to reach their A than founders are budgeting for.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an angel setting valuations and terms at seed stage, the A-round milestone is the exit ramp your portfolio companies need to reach. If the A bar has moved up, your seed company's survival runway math needs to be stress-tested against the new standard — and your due diligence should probe burn efficiency harder than it did two years ago.
