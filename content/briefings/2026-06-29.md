### Baseten Raises $1.5B Series F at $13B — the AI Inference Layer Is the Week's Hottest Trade

**Baseten** raised a **$1.5B Series F at a $13B valuation**, led by Altimeter, Conviction and Spark Capital with Sands Capital and Wellington co-leading — completed across two tranches (at $13B and $11B) and its *fourth raise in 18 months*, on the back of roughly 20x year-over-year revenue growth and more than a billion inference requests a day. The round is the clearest sign yet that the money chasing AI has moved decisively from the model labs to the **inference layer** — the unglamorous plumbing that actually serves models in production.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Angel Investor, this is the picks-and-shovels thesis in its purest form — when the flagship category (frontier models) is too capital-intensive to enter, the durable returns hide in the layer everyone *running* a model has to pay for. As an Applied AI Leader, it's also direct architecture intelligence for NWA Intelligence: serving cost and latency are becoming the real moat, and "who hosts the inference" is now a board-level question for any AI-heavy company you advise.

**Suggested action:** Use Baseten's raise as a frame to pressure-test NWA's AI infra sourcing — the inference layer is consolidating fast and richly priced, so the angel edge is in the *adjacent* plays (eval, observability, cost-routing) that the megarounds depend on but haven't yet absorbed.

### OpenAI Previews GPT-5.6 (Sol, Terra, Luna) — Under Government-Approved Access Only

On **June 26**, OpenAI opened a limited preview of its next-generation **GPT-5.6 family — flagship Sol, mid-tier Terra, and fast/cheap Luna** — initially to only ~20 government-approved companies. Sol set a new state-of-the-art on Terminal-Bench 2.1 (agentic coding and command-line work) and ships with OpenAI's strongest safety stack to date — a launch posture that, coming two weeks after the government's Fable/Mythos export standoff, makes "gated, vetted rollout" the new normal at the frontier.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Applied AI Leader, a coding-and-agent SOTA from OpenAI is a live benchmark for the Claude-centric stack you're building on — worth tracking on capability, but the *access model* (government-vetted allowlist) is the more strategic signal: frontier capability and frontier availability are now separate variables you have to architect around. As an AI Educator, the "preview to 20 approved firms" rollout is a ready-made FAU case on how compute and capability are being governed, not just sold.

### Anthropic Accuses Alibaba of Illicitly Harvesting Claude via ~25,000 Fake Accounts

Anthropic alleged that operators linked to Alibaba's **Qwen** lab ran roughly **28.8 million queries through nearly 25,000 fraudulent accounts** between April and June to harvest Claude's software-engineering and agentic-reasoning behavior — "adversarial distillation" that sidesteps China geo-restrictions — and said it has briefed the White House and senators urging tighter safeguards. (Separately, the Commerce Department this week authorized a limited re-release of Anthropic's restricted **Mythos 5** to ~100 vetted US firms and agencies, while Fable 5 stays barred — the export saga continues.)

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Applied AI Leader betting NWA Intelligence on Claude as durable plumbing, this is a reminder that the model you depend on is now the target of state-scale extraction and counter-measures — account-integrity and rate-limit regimes will tighten, which can ripple into how you provision and authenticate your own automated Claude usage. As a Board Director, "is our AI vendor's IP — and our access to it — defensible against this kind of distillation" is becoming a real diligence question.

## Section C — Trends to Watch

### The "Giga VC" Concentration Is Reshaping How Capital Flows

H1 2026 data crystallized a pattern: **median seed pre-money is up ~19% to ~$16M while seed deal volume fell ~29% year-over-year** — a flight to quality — even as roughly **six firms (a16z, Thrive, Lightspeed, Sequoia, Founders Fund, General Catalyst) now dominate startup financing** end-to-end. Fewer, larger, pricier rounds concentrated among a handful of mega-funds, with AI startups commanding a ~$1M premium over the market median.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Angel Investor, this is the structural backdrop to every deal NWA sees — when the Giga VCs own the priced, late-stage game and seed gets pricier and more selective, the angel edge moves to the *earlier, un-syndicated, off-thesis* names the megafunds haven't reached yet. As a Board Director, it reframes follow-on math: if a winner attracts a Giga VC at the next round, your pro-rata and signaling strategy has to be set *before* that gravity takes over.

### Enterprise Agents Are Hardening Into a Standard Software Layer

The agent story this week was about *adoption infrastructure*, not new models: **NVIDIA opened an enterprise AI-agent toolkit with 16+ major software partners** (Adobe, Atlassian, SAP, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Siemens), agent-software spend is forecast to hit **~$206B in 2026 (up ~139% YoY)** — yet a Fivetran readiness index found **only ~15% of organizations are production-ready** while ~60% are already spending millions. The gap between agent investment and agent readiness is the real story.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Applied AI Leader, the "everyone's buying, few are ready" gap is both the risk and the opening — the orgs that solve production-readiness (eval, governance, integration) capture the budget everyone else is wasting, which is precisely NWA Intelligence's lane. As an AI Educator, that 15%-ready / 60%-spending split is the single best slide you can hand FAU leaders to separate AI hype from operational reality.

## Section D — Learn and Watch

- **📝 Talk/Essay — MIT "AI and Society Forum": David Autor reframes AI's threat as *expertise scarcity*, not job loss (June 23)** — MIT economist David Autor argued that AI's labor impact turns on whether it *erodes or preserves the scarcity of human expertise*: automating routine support work can elevate the expert, but automating expert tasks devalues them. The sharpest economic framing yet of the "what is human judgment still worth" question — a ready-made spine for an FAU lecture or a Medium piece. [[MIT News](https://news.mit.edu/2026/exploring-societal-impacts-of-ai-0623)]
