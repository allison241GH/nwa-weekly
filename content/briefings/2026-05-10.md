### Anthropic Signs Massive Compute Deal with SpaceX (xAI)

Anthropic secured access to SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center — over 300 megawatts and 220,000 NVIDIA GPUs — immediately doubling Claude API and Pro/Max rate limits. The deal includes future plans for orbital AI compute capacity. This is the largest single compute partnership in AI history, following Musk's merger of xAI into SpaceX earlier this year.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** Compute access is rapidly becoming the defining moat in AI, not just model quality. As an AI leader building with Claude, this means more reliable throughput for your CoWork workflows. As an investor, it signals that infrastructure partnerships — not just model research — are where competitive positioning is being decided.

*Talking point for FAU: The AI industry's infrastructure layer is consolidating faster than the application layer.*

### Robinhood Files RVII — Venture Capital Goes Retail

Robinhood confidentially filed with the SEC for Ventures Fund II (RVII), targeting early-stage and growth-stage startups. This follows RVI's success — the first fund attracted 150,000+ retail investors, doubled from its $21 IPO price to $43.69, and holds stakes in OpenAI, Stripe, Databricks, and Ramp. RVII will cast a wider net into earlier-stage companies.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** This is a structural shift in venture investing. Retail-accessible venture vehicles will change deal dynamics, co-investment opportunities, and how founders think about cap tables. Understanding how these vehicles interact with traditional angel rounds is essential for NWA — see this week's VC coaching section.

*Track RVII's portfolio composition when disclosed — it may surface deal flow opportunities.*

### Nuro Gets California Driverless Permit for 35K-Vehicle Uber Robotaxi Fleet

Nuro secured a California DMV driverless testing permit for Lucid Gravity SUVs at speeds up to 45 mph, day or night, in Santa Clara and San Mateo counties. Uber expanded its Lucid investment to $500M (11.5% ownership) and raised its fleet commitment 75% to at least 35,000 robotaxis. Bay Area launch targeted for late 2026.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As a former automotive CPO, this is the most vertically integrated robotaxi deployment path yet — Uber's distribution network + Lucid's vehicle platform + Nuro's autonomy stack. The 35K vehicle commitment signals genuine commercial conviction, not just a pilot.
