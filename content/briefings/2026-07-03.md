### AI's Biggest Checks Moved to the Plumbing — Together AI ($800M) and Joulent ($1.75B)

The week's largest venture rounds weren't models or apps — they were **infrastructure and the power to run it**. **Together AI raised an $800M Series C at an $8.3B valuation** (led by Aramco Ventures) for its GPU-cloud platform that lets enterprises train and serve open-source models, while **Joulent raised $1.75B** (National Grid Ventures) to build energy infrastructure for AI and compute-intensive industries — the single biggest round of the week. The signal is unmistakable: the money chasing AI has moved decisively to the layer everyone *running* a model has to pay for, now stretching all the way down to the electrons.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Angel Investor, this is the picks-and-shovels thesis in its purest, and now widest, form — the frontier model race is too capital-intensive to enter, so durable returns hide in compute, serving, and increasingly *power*, the constraint no AI company can engineer around. As an Applied AI Leader, it's direct architecture intelligence for NWA Intelligence: inference cost and energy availability are becoming the real moat, and "who hosts this and what does it cost to run" is now a board-level question for any AI-heavy company you advise.

**Suggested action:** Use this week's tape to pressure-test NWA's AI-infra sourcing — the compute and energy layers are richly priced and crowded with megafunds, so the angel edge is in the *adjacent* plays (eval, cost-routing, grid-adjacent efficiency) the megarounds depend on but haven't yet absorbed.

### Anthropic Launches Claude Science — and Will Run Its Own Drug-Discovery Programs

On **June 30**, Anthropic released **Claude Science**, an AI workbench for researchers and drugmakers that automates biology and chemistry tasks (predicting protein structures, querying 60+ scientific databases) — and, more strikingly, announced it will run **its own preclinical drug-discovery programs** focused on neglected and rare diseases. A frontier lab is moving up the value chain from tool-maker to drug developer.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Applied AI Leader, this is a strategic tell about where AI margins are headed — labs are no longer content to sell the shovel; they want equity in what the shovel digs up, which reframes the "platform vs. application" question for everyone building on top of them. As an Angel Investor, vertical AI-for-science just became a legible investable category, and as an AI Educator it's a ready-made FAU case on how a model company becomes a discovery company.

### Claude Fable 5 Returns Globally as US Lifts Export Controls — With a New Jailbreak Classifier

After the June export-control standoff, the **US lifted its restrictions on June 30**, and **Fable 5 came back globally on July 1** across the Claude platform, Claude.ai, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork. Anthropic paired the relaunch with a new safety **classifier** trained to detect and block the specific jailbreak technique behind the original restriction — it now stops that technique in **more than 99% of attempts**.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Applied AI Leader betting NWA Intelligence on Claude as durable plumbing, you get your top creative model back *plus* a hardened safety layer — but the whole episode confirms that model *availability*, not just capability, is now a governed variable you have to architect around. As a Board Director, "can our AI vendor guarantee continuity of access under shifting export rules" has become a real diligence line, not a hypothetical.

## Section C — Trends to Watch

### Seed Has Bifurcated Into a Flight to Quality

H1 2026 data crystallized the pattern: **median seed pre-money is up ~19% to ~$16M while seed deal volume fell ~29% year-over-year** — fewer, pricier, more selective rounds — with **AI startups carrying a 1.3–1.6x valuation premium** over non-AI peers. The market is paying up for a shrinking set of "obviously fundable" companies while everything else struggles to clear the bar.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Angel Investor, this is the structural backdrop to every deal NWA sees — when the priciest, most competitive seed rounds cluster around AI-native teams with early traction, the angel edge moves to the *earlier, off-thesis, un-syndicated* names before the flight-to-quality gravity reprices them. As a Board Director, it reframes runway math: a portfolio company that isn't in the "obviously fundable" bucket needs 24+ months of cash, because the next round won't come easy or fast.

### Embodied AI Is Quietly Crossing Into Production Scale

The humanoid story shifted from demos to throughput: **Figure is producing its Figure 03 at ~one robot per hour** at its BotQ factory, **Tesla is converting Fremont lines for Optimus Gen 3** with a summer ramp, and **China now has 150+ humanoid firms to roughly 20 in the US**. The unsolved milestone — unsupervised operation in unstructured environments — hasn't been crossed, but manufacturing capacity is being built ahead of it.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As a former automotive CPO, you've watched this exact playbook — build the line before the product is fully proven, then ramp into demand — and the China-vs-US firm count is the more strategic signal for where embodied-AI supply chains will concentrate. As an Angel Investor, the investable edge is less the humanoids themselves than the *components and tooling* (actuators, tactile sensing, sim-to-real data) that every one of those 150+ firms needs.

### The Enterprise-Agent Spending Gap Keeps Widening

Agent-software spend is forecast at **~$206B in 2026 (up ~139% YoY)** and Gartner expects **40% of enterprise apps to embed task-specific agents by year-end** — yet production reality lags badly: most deployments are still narrow triage, planner, and narrator agents, and readiness surveys keep finding only a small minority of orgs truly production-ready. The gap between agent *investment* and agent *readiness* is the real story.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an Applied AI Leader, the "everyone's buying, few are ready" gap is both the risk and the opening — the orgs that solve production-readiness (eval, governance, integration) capture the budget everyone else is wasting, which is precisely NWA Intelligence's lane. As an AI Educator, that spend-vs-readiness split is the single cleanest slide to separate AI hype from operational reality for FAU leaders.
