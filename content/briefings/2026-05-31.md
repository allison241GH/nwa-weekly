Investors include Altimeter, Sequoia, Coatue, GIC, and strategic backers Micron, Samsung, and SK Hynix.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an applied AI leader and Claude Code power user, the dynamic workflows feature changes what's possible in your NWA Intelligence builds this week — 1,000 subagents running in parallel is in production today, not on a roadmap. ## Section C — Trends to Watch

### AI Hardware: Investors Betting Mega-Rounds Before the Product Ships

Brett Adcock — founder of Figure AI and Archer Aviation — raised a $700M Series A at a $6B valuation for Hark, a "universal AI interface" startup building agentic personal AI on custom hardware. The company had no public product at close. Backers include NVIDIA, Qualcomm Ventures, ARK Invest, Intel Capital, and Salesforce Ventures. This continues a distinct pattern: investors are writing checks that would have funded late-stage companies, to proven AI hardware founders, before a single unit ships.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an angel investor, this signals a bifurcation in the market — mega pre-product rounds going to proven founders, while first-time founders face a rising Series A bar ($2M+ ARR, strong burn efficiency). Knowing which game you're evaluating in each deal changes your diligence approach entirely.

### Enterprise AI Agent Adoption Crossing the Tipping Point

Gartner projects 40% of enterprise applications will integrate AI agents by end of 2026, up from less than 5% in 2025. Capgemini reports 93% of business leaders say companies that scale AI agents in the next 12 months will gain a durable competitive edge. The shift from "AI feature" to "AI infrastructure" is happening at enterprise velocity — not in a future product cycle.

**Why it matters to Jamie:** As an AI educator and board director, this adoption curve is your teaching thesis made measurable — your FAU students and board companies are entering the year where AI agent implementation separates winners from followers. The window to teach this practically, with empirical data, is now.
